#Vietnam–US Trade & Tariff Impact Analysis
#Overview

This project analyzes the potential impact of new U.S. tariff policies on Vietnam’s export economy, especially under the scenario where Vietnamese goods may face tariffs up to 46% in the U.S. market. The study combines trade data, tariff policies, and macroeconomic indicators to evaluate risks, identify vulnerable industries, and recommend strategic market diversification solutions.

#Objectives
Evaluate the impact of U.S. tariff policies on Vietnam’s exports.
Identify export sectors most vulnerable to tariff increases.
Analyze Vietnam’s dependence on the U.S. market.
Recommend alternative export markets and long-term trade strategies.
Support decision-making through data analytics and visualization.

Key Insights
1. Vietnam is Highly Dependent on the U.S. Market

The United States accounts for over 30% of Vietnam’s total exports, making Vietnam highly vulnerable to changes in U.S. trade policies.

2. A 46% Tariff Could Severely Impact Vietnam’s Economy

The estimated export loss exceeds 12% of total exports, threatening:

GDP growth
Employment
Manufacturing output
Supply chain stability
3. Vietnam Relies Strongly on Global Supply Chains

Vietnam imports large volumes of:

Machinery
Electronic components
Steel
Textile materials

This highlights Vietnam’s strength in manufacturing and assembly, but also reveals dependency on foreign suppliers.

4. Technology Products Now Dominate Exports

High-tech products such as electronics and machinery now surpass traditional labor-intensive industries, signaling Vietnam’s transition toward higher-value manufacturing.

5. Market Diversification is Critical

The EU, Japan, China, and India are identified as the most promising alternative markets due to:

Large population
Strong purchasing power
Low tariff barriers
Stable import demand
Strategic Recommendations
Diversify export destinations beyond the U.S.
Leverage FTAs such as EVFTA and ASEAN agreements
Move up the value chain:
OEM → ODM → Own Brand
Increase localization and supporting industries
Improve product quality and international compliance standards
Strengthen resilience against geopolitical and trade-policy risks
Limitations
Product grouping was simplified using keyword classification.
Tariff impact estimates are scenario-based assumptions.
External macroeconomic data may contain reporting delays.
